# Venue Selection Guide

## Goal

Choose the venue that maximizes impact while matching your paper's contribution type, maturity, and timeline.

## Decision Factors (in priority order)

### 1. Contribution Type
| Your Paper Has... | Best Venue Type |
|---|---|
| One clear technical insight | Top conference (NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, ACL, SIGGRAPH, OSDI) |
| Thorough exploration of a method | Journal (TPAMI, JMLR, TOIS, Nature Methods) |
| Novel system / tool | Systems conference (USENIX ATC, EuroSys, NSDI, SIGMOD) |
| New dataset or benchmark | Dataset track at major conference or NeurIPS Datasets |
| Position / vision | Workshop paper, position paper at CSCW, CHI |
| Negative results | Workshop, TMLR, or dedicated negative-result venues |

### 2. Timeline
- **Conference**: Submission 4-8 months before presentation. Rejection = resubmit to next cycle.
- **Journal**: Rolling submissions, 3-12 month review. Rejection = resubmit elsewhere.
- **Workshop**: 1-3 months before event. Higher acceptance rate, lower prestige.
- **arXiv only**: Immediate. No peer review. Good for establishing priority.

### 3. Acceptance Rate (approximate)
| Tier | Rate | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Top conference | 15-25% | NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, ACL, SIGCOMM, OSDI |
| Strong conference | 25-35% | EMNLP, ECCV, AAAI, IJCAI, USENIX ATC |
| Solid conference | 35-50% | WACV, NAACL, ICRA |
| Workshop | 40-70% | Associated with top conferences |
| Journal | Varies widely | TPAMI (~15%), TMLR (rolling, desk-reject filter) |

### 4. Audience
- **CS Theory**: STOC, FOCS, SODA
- **ML/AI**: NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, AAAI
- **Computer Vision**: CVPR, ICCV, ECCV
- **NLP**: ACL, EMNLP, NAACL
- **Systems**: OSDI, SOSP, EuroSys, NSDI, USENIX ATC
- **Databases**: SIGMOD, VLDB, ICDE
- **Security**: IEEE S&P, CCS, USENIX Security, NDSS
- **HCI**: CHI, UIST, CSCW
- **Robotics**: ICRA, IROS, RSS
- **Graphics**: SIGGRAPH, SIGGRAPH Asia
- **Interdisciplinary**: Nature, Science, PNAS (for high-impact breakthroughs)

## Venue Selection Workflow

1. Identify your paper's primary contribution type
2. List 3-5 candidate venues at decreasing selectivity
3. Check upcoming deadlines on the venue's website
4. Read 3-4 accepted papers from each candidate to verify fit
5. Choose the most selective venue where your paper would be in the top 50% of accepted papers
6. Have a backup venue ready before submitting

## Red Flags

- Submitting to a venue you have never read papers from
- Choosing by deadline proximity rather than fit
- Ignoring page limits (a 14-page paper does not fit an 8-page venue)
- Assuming workshop = easy acceptance (some workshops are highly selective)
- Not checking the venue's formatting requirements before writing

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Paper matches the venue's topic scope
- [ ] Paper length fits within page limits
- [ ] Contribution type matches venue expectations
- [ ] Backup venue identified
- [ ] Deadline confirmed on official website (not third-party aggregator)
- [ ] Template downloaded and verified
