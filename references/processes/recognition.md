# Recognition  --  The 12-Month Plan to Be Known

## Goal

Not just to publish. To be recognized. Papers, conferences, magazine articles, investors  --  these are not separate goals. They are one integrated flywheel. This guide shows you how to spin it.

## The Flywheel

```
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                             │
    ▼                                             │
  Paper ──→ Conference Talk ──→ Magazine Article  │
    │             │                 │             │
    │             ▼                 ▼             │
    │       Reputation       Public Audience      │
    │             │                 │             │
    │             └────────┬────────┘             │
    │                      ▼                      │
    └────────── Investor Interest ────────────────┘
```

Each output feeds the next. A paper gets you a talk. A talk gets you noticed by a journalist. A magazine article gets you investor inbound. Investor interest funds the next paper.

## Phase 1: The Foundation (Months 1-3)

### Month 1: The Paper
- [ ] Run the ideation diagnostic. Your idea must pass the Shannon filter.
- [ ] Write the paper using `references/sections/` guides.
- [ ] Submit to the best venue you can. Aim high. If rejected, you have a polished preprint.
- [ ] Post on arXiv, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Hacker News.

### Month 2: The Talk
- [ ] Submit the paper to 2-3 conference workshops or local meetups.
- [ ] Prepare the 15-minute talk. Practice until you don't need slides.
- [ ] Record the talk. Post it. A recorded talk is a permanent asset.
- [ ] Write a blog post version. Same content, different format. See `references/formats/blogpost.md`.

### Month 3: The Amplification
- [ ] Identify 5 people whose work you cite. Email them. "I cited your work on X. Would love your thoughts on our approach."
- [ ] Respond to every comment on your blog post and social posts.
- [ ] Submit a 3-sentence pitch to a magazine editor. See `references/formats/magazine.md`.

---

## Phase 2: The Breakout (Months 4-8)

### Month 4-5: The Magazine Article
- [ ] Magazines operate on 2-3 month lead times. Pitch early.
- [ ] Write the article differently from the paper. No equations. No jargon. One human story.
- [ ] The magazine article reaches people who will never read your paper. That's the point.

### Month 6-7: The Investor Conversations
- [ ] Prepare the pitch deck. See `references/formats/pitchdeck.md`.
- [ ] Warm introductions only. Cold emails to investors have a 0.1% response rate.
- [ ] Use the magazine article and conference talk as social proof. "As covered in [Magazine]..."
- [ ] The ask: "We are not raising yet. We would love your feedback on our direction."

### Month 8: The Second Paper
- [ ] You now have more data, more credibility, and more confidence.
- [ ] Cite your own first paper. This is not vanity  --  it's building a research program.
- [ ] The second paper should answer one of the "Open Problems" from the first.

---

## Phase 3: The Institution (Months 9-12)

### Month 9-10: The Invited Talk
- [ ] Conference workshops lead to conference invitations.
- [ ] Say yes to every invitation. Every talk is recorded and shared.
- [ ] Prepare the keynote version: 45 minutes, deeper, with a story arc.

### Month 11-12: The Dossier and The Raise
- [ ] Build the complete dossier. See `references/processes/dossier.md`.
- [ ] Paper + magazine + blog post + talk recording + pitch deck + press kit.
- [ ] If fundraising: run a process. 20 meetings. One term sheet.
- [ ] If not fundraising: you now have a dossier that makes you the obvious expert.

## The Content Machine

One insight → repackaged for every format:

| Format | Purpose | Time to Create |
|---|---|---|
| Research paper | Credibility, depth, peer validation | 2 months |
| Blog post | Reach, accessibility, SEO | 2 days |
| Conference talk | Network, reputation, recording | 1 week prep |
| Magazine article | Mainstream credibility, investor inbound | 2 weeks |
| Pitch deck | Fundraising, partnerships | 1 week |
| Twitter/X thread | Virality, top-of-funnel | 2 hours |
| Podcast appearance | Warm audience, depth | 1 hour |

**Rule**: One insight. Seven formats. Twelve months. At the end of the flywheel, you are not just published. You are recognized.

## How to Pitch Magazine Editors

Magazine articles are PITCHED, not submitted. The pitch is 3 sentences:

```
Sentence 1: The HOOK — a surprising fact, a provocative question, or a
            "did you know..." that creates curiosity.
Sentence 2: The STORY — what happened, what changed, why it matters.
Sentence 3: The CREDENTIAL — why YOU are the person to write this.
```

Example:
```
"92% of developers use AI coding tools, but a structural flaw means
every AI-generated line of code is reviewed by the same architecture
that wrote it — like a proofreader who cannot see their own typos.
We built a system that eliminates this blind spot by using two
independent AI models that review each other's work, and it produces
measurably better code than any single model can. I led this research
at [Institution] and would love to write a 1500-word piece explaining
why this matters for every developer."
```

Send to the editor whose section matches your topic. Follow up once after 2 weeks. No response = pitch a different editor or a different magazine.
