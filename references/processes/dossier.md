# Dossier  --  Your Complete Professional Package

## Goal

A dossier is the complete package that makes you the obvious choice  --  for funding, for a job, for a speaking invitation, for a partnership. It's what you send when someone says "tell me more about what you do."

A paper alone is not a dossier. A pitch deck alone is not a dossier. A dossier is the full arsenal, ready to deploy instantly.

## What Goes in a Dossier

### 1. The One-Pager (Executive Summary)
One page that answers everything:
- What problem do you solve?
- What is your solution?
- What is the evidence it works?
- What are you asking for? (Funding, partnership, invitation, job)
- Who are you?

This is read in 60 seconds. It must stand alone. If they read nothing else, they should understand your work.

### 2. The Research Paper or Technical Document
The deep evidence. Your peer-reviewed paper, white paper, or technical report.
- PDF, professionally formatted.
- If unpublished: preprint on arXiv with a clean PDF.

### 3. The Pitch Deck (If Fundraising)
The 12-slide story. See `references/formats/pitchdeck.md`.
- The deck is a LEAVE-BEHIND, not a presentation.
- It must work without you in the room.

### 4. The Talk Recording
A recorded conference talk or pitch.
- 15-20 minutes. Posted on YouTube or Vimeo.
- Title: the one-sentence insight.
- Description: links to paper, website, and contact.

### 5. The Magazine or Blog Article
The accessible version of your work.
- Published in a magazine, or on your blog, or on Medium.
- 800-2000 words. Readable by a smart non-expert.

### 6. The Press Kit
A folder with:
- High-resolution headshot (professional, not a selfie).
- High-resolution project images, diagrams, screenshots.
- 1-paragraph bio (50 words).
- 3-paragraph bio (150 words).
- Full CV.
- Company logo (vector format, SVG or EPS).
- Key statistics (1-3 numbers that summarize your impact).

### 7. The Social Proof Collection
- Quotes from users, collaborators, or notable people.
- Logos of institutions you've worked with or been featured in.
- "As seen in"  --  magazines, podcasts, conferences.
- Awards, grants, recognitions.

### 8. The Website or Landing Page
- One page. Your name, your one-sentence insight, links to everything above.
- A photo. A contact form or email.
- If you have a product: a "get started" button.

## The Dossier File Structure

```
dossier/
├── 01-executive-summary.pdf        (1 page)
├── 02-research-paper.pdf           (8-14 pages)
├── 03-pitch-deck.pdf               (12 slides)
├── 04-talk-recording.url           (YouTube link)
├── 05-magazine-article.pdf         (or .url)
├── 06-blog-post.pdf                (or .url)
├── press-kit/
│   ├── headshot.jpg
│   ├── diagram-1.png
│   ├── diagram-2.png
│   ├── logo.svg
│   ├── bio-short.txt
│   ├── bio-long.txt
│   └── CV.pdf
├── social-proof/
│   ├── quotes.txt
│   ├── logos/
│   │   ├── institution-1.png
│   │   └── institution-2.png
│   └── as-seen-in.txt
└── README.md                       (How to use this dossier)
```

## When to Use the Dossier

| Situation | What to Send |
|---|---|
| Warm intro to an investor | One-pager + pitch deck + paper |
| Speaking invitation request | One-pager + talk recording + bio |
| Job application (research role) | One-pager + paper + CV + talk recording |
| Press inquiry | One-pager + press kit + magazine article |
| Partnership conversation | One-pager + paper + pitch deck |
| Award or grant nomination | One-pager + paper + social proof |
| "Tell me more about what you do" | One-pager + link to full dossier |
