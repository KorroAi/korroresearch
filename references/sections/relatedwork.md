# Related Work Writing Guide

## Goal

Position your work relative to the existing literature. Show you understand the field and that your work fills a genuine gap.

## Organization

### Thematic (Recommended)
Group papers by technical approach, not by date. Each paragraph covers one line of work.
```
[Approach category]: Method A, Method B, and Method C all use [technique].
They achieve [results] but share a limitation: [what they cannot do].
Our method addresses this by [how we differ].
```

### Chronological (Use sparingly)
Only when showing historical progression of a specific technique.

## Writing Rules

1. **Never list papers without synthesis** - Group them by approach and identify patterns
2. **Be fair but critical** - Acknowledge strengths, identify genuine limitations
3. **Cite the original** - Not a survey that mentions it
4. **Keep it focused** - Only cite work relevant to YOUR contribution
5. **End with the gap** - Each paragraph should make clear what remains unsolved
6. **Distinguish your work** - The final paragraph(s) should explicitly contrast your method against the closest prior work

## Common Structures

### Structure A: Broad to narrow
1. Broad area overview
2. Closest sub area
3. Most related methods
4. Explicit contrast with your method

### Structure B: Problem centric
1. Methods addressing problem aspect 1
2. Methods addressing problem aspect 2
3. Methods attempting full solution
4. Why yours is different

### Structure C: Technique centric
1. Methods using technique X (related to your component 1)
2. Methods using technique Y (related to your component 2)
3. Methods combining X and Y
4. Your novel combination

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Papers are grouped thematically, not listed chronologically
- [ ] Each paragraph ends with a clear gap or limitation
- [ ] Your method is explicitly contrasted with closest prior work
- [ ] Citations are to original sources
- [ ] No straw man arguments against prior work
- [ ] Recent papers (last 2 3 years) are well represented
