# Conclusion Writing Guide

## Goal

Close the paper with impact. Remind readers what they learned, acknowledge limits, and point forward.

## Structure

### 1. Summary (1 paragraph)
- Restate the problem and your solution
- Summarize key results with numbers
- Do NOT copy paste the abstract

### 2. Contributions (3 5 bullets or 1 paragraph)
- What new knowledge does the reader have now?
- Be specific: "We showed that X improves Y by Z%"

### 3. Limitations (1 paragraph)
- Honest acknowledgment of what the method cannot do
- Specific failure cases or scenarios
- This builds credibility with reviewers

### 4. Future Work (1 paragraph)
- Natural extensions of the current work
- New questions opened by your results
- Avoid vague "more research is needed" - be specific

## Writing Rules

1. Do not introduce new claims or results
2. Do not copy paste sentences from abstract or introduction
3. Be specific about limitations - generic "our method has limitations" is worse than naming them
4. Future work should be actionable, not aspirational
5. End with impact: remind the reader why this matters

## Common Mistakes
- Restating the abstract verbatim
- Introducing unsupported claims
- Overclaiming contributions
- Vague future work ("more research is needed")
- Skipping limitations to appear stronger (reviewers see through this)

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Summary is distinct from abstract in phrasing
- [ ] Results are summarized with specific numbers
- [ ] Limitations are honestly discussed
- [ ] Future work is specific and actionable
- [ ] No new claims introduced
- [ ] Final sentence leaves a strong impression
