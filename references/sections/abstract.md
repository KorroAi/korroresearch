# Abstract Writing Guide

## Goal

Write a strong abstract by doing three things:
1. Think through the abstract logic first
2. Follow one of the three templates below
3. Revise multiple times

## Pre Writing Questions

Answer these before writing:
1. What technical problem do we solve, and why is there no well established solution?
2. What is our technical contribution?
3. Why can our method work in essence?
4. What technical advantage and new insight do we provide?

## Template A: Challenge -> Contribution

Use when you have one clear contribution that directly addresses the challenge.

### Structure
1. **Task** - One sentence defining what output from what input
2. **Technical challenge** - What previous methods fail at
3. **Contribution** - One to two sentences naming your method and how it solves the challenge (do not explain every step, just name the key technical term)
4. **Benefits** - What advantage this gives
5. **Experiment summary** - One sentence on key results

### Expert Notes
- Discuss previous work around the challenge you actually solve
- The technical term must be easy to understand - no reader should feel a jump
- Do not explain every detailed step in the contribution sentence

## Template B: Challenge -> Insight -> Contribution

Use when your core contribution comes from a specific insight.

### Structure
1. **Task**
2. **Technical challenge** for previous methods
3. **Insight** - One sentence revealing the key realization
4. **Contribution** - One to two sentences implementing the insight
5. **Benefits** of technical novelty
6. **Experiment summary**

### Expert Notes
- The insight sentence is the most important - make it crisp
- The implementation sentence names the technique, not every detail
- Readers must understand both why the insight matters and how you implemented it

## Template C: Multiple Contributions

Use when you have 2 3 distinct contributions.

### Structure
1. **Task**
2. **Contrast sentence** about prior methods (optional)
3. **Contribution 1** + its technical advantage (one sentence)
4. **Contribution 2** + its technical advantage
5. **Contribution 3** + its technical advantage
6. **Experiment summary**

### Expert Notes
- Express "contribution + advantage" in ONE sentence per contribution
- This is a critical skill for writing good abstracts
- If contributions build on each other, make the chain clear

## Abstract Quality Checklist

1. Can a reader identify task, challenge, insight/contribution, and results in ONE pass?
2. Are all major claims supported by experiments?
3. Are technical names self contained and readable?
4. Is there any sentence that mixes too many messages?
5. Is the abstract under the venue word limit (typically 150 250 words)?
